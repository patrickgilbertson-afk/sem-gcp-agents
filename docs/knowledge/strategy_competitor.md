# Competitor Campaign Strategy

## Objectives

1. **Conquest**: Capture users researching competitors
2. **Positioning**: Differentiate our offering vs. competitors
3. **ROI Balance**: Accept higher CPA for strategic value

## Target Metrics

- **CPA**: $120-$180 (higher than NonBrand, acceptable for conquest)
- **Conversion Rate**: 3-5% (lower than brand/NonBrand, expected)
- **Quality Score**: 5-7 average (expected lower QS on competitor terms)
- **CTR**: 2-4% (lower than NonBrand, but reasonable)
- **Impression Share**: 30-50% (opportunistic, not 100% coverage)

## Philosophy

Competitor campaigns are **INDIVIDUAL** (not synced across geos):
- Each competitor has unique market position in each geo
- Budget allocation is opportunistic, not strategic
- Focus on high-value wins, not comprehensive coverage

## Competitor Tier List

### Tier 1: Primary Competitors (Highest Budget Priority)

#### GitHub Copilot
**Strength**: Brand recognition, Microsoft backing
**Weakness**: Privacy concerns (code sent to cloud), expensive for teams
**Our Differentiator**: Better privacy controls, more affordable for teams, superior context awareness

**Keywords**:
- "github copilot alternative"
- "github copilot vs [our product]"
- "github copilot pricing" (price comparison searchers)
- "github copilot privacy concerns"

#### Cursor
**Strength**: Modern UX, fast performance
**Weakness**: Limited language support, smaller team
**Our Differentiator**: Better multi-language support, more mature product, enterprise features

**Keywords**:
- "cursor ai alternative"
- "cursor vs [our product]"
- "cursor pricing"

#### Tabnine
**Strength**: On-premise option, enterprise focus
**Weakness**: Older technology, lower accuracy
**Our Differentiator**: More powerful AI models, better UX, more active development

**Keywords**:
- "tabnine alternative"
- "tabnine vs [our product]"
- "better than tabnine"

### Tier 2: Secondary Competitors (Lower Budget)

- **Codeium**: Growing competitor, free tier focus
- **Amazon Q**: Enterprise focus, AWS ecosystem
- **Replit AI**: Educational/beginner focus

## Keyword Strategy

### Comparison Keywords (Highest Intent)
- "[Competitor] alternative"
- "[Competitor] vs [our product]"
- "better than [competitor]"
- "[our product] vs [competitor]"

### Pricing/Feature Keywords
- "[Competitor] pricing" (price-sensitive searchers)
- "[Competitor] review" (research phase, lower intent)
- "[Competitor] free alternative"

### Problem Keywords
- "[Competitor] not working" (frustrated users, opportunistic)
- "[Competitor] limitations"
- "[Competitor] privacy"

**Negative Keywords**:
- "download" (looking for the competitor's product)
- "tutorial" (already using competitor)
- "how to use [competitor]" (committed users)
- "jobs at [competitor]" (job seekers)
- "discount code" (unlikely to switch)

## Ad Copy Guidelines

### Headline Strategy

**Direct Comparison**:
- "Better than [Competitor] | [Our Product]"
- "Switch from [Competitor] | Save 30%"
- "[Our Product] vs [Competitor] - Try Free"

**Problem/Solution**:
- "Frustrated with [Competitor]? Try [Our Product]"
- "[Competitor] Alternative | More Features, Lower Cost"

**Differentiator-Focused**:
- "Better Privacy than [Competitor]"
- "10x Faster than [Competitor]"
- "Team Plans from $35/user (vs $39 at [Competitor])"

### Description Strategy

**Comparison Table Format**:
- "[Our Product]: $35/user. [Competitor]: $39/user"
- "[Our Product]: Full project context. [Competitor]: Single file only"

**Social Proof**:
- "500K+ developers switched from [Competitor]"
- "Rated higher than [Competitor] on G2"

**Risk Reversal**:
- "Try free for 14 days. Easy migration from [Competitor]"
- "Import your [Competitor] settings in 1 click"

**Example Ad**:
```
Switch from GitHub Copilot - Save 30%
Better Privacy. More Context. Lower Cost. Try Free 14 Days.
Trusted by 500K+ Developers. Easy Migration from Copilot.
```

## Bidding Strategy

**Target CPA with Higher Tolerance**:
- Accept CPA up to $180 (1.5x NonBrand target)
- Competitor conquest has strategic value beyond direct ROI
- Prevents competitor from capturing 100% of their own branded traffic

**Impression Share Targets**:
- Don't aim for 100% (too expensive, diminishing returns)
- Target 30-50% impression share
- Focus on top positions when we do show (pos 1-2)

**Budget Allocation**:
- 10% of total budget to competitor campaigns
- Within that 10%:
  - GitHub: 40% (largest competitor)
  - Cursor: 30%
  - Tabnine: 20%
  - Others: 10%

## Quality Score Challenges

**Expected Lower QS** (5-7 average):
- Google penalizes ads on competitor terms
- Lower expected CTR (users searching for competitor, not us)
- Ad relevance harder to achieve (can't use competitor name in ad in some cases)

**How to Improve**:
1. **Landing Pages**: Create dedicated "[Competitor] Alternative" pages
2. **Ad Relevance**: Use "alternative", "vs", "better than" phrasing
3. **Negative Keywords**: Aggressively filter non-switchers ("download", "tutorial")

## Legal Considerations

**Allowed** (per Google Ads policy):
- Bidding on competitor brand terms
- Using competitor names in ad copy (in comparison context)
- "Alternative to [Competitor]"

**NOT Allowed**:
- Trademark infringement (claiming to BE the competitor)
- Misleading comparisons (false claims)
- Using competitor logos in ad creative

**Best Practice**:
- Always use "alternative", "vs", or comparison language
- Never imply affiliation with competitor
- Have legal review copy before launch

## Geo-Specific Considerations

### US
- Most competitive market
- All major competitors active
- Highest budgets

### UK
- Similar dynamics to US
- Slightly lower competition
- Focus on Tier 1 competitors only

### DE/FR
- Lower competitor ad activity
- More opportunity for conquest
- May need localized comparison pages

### Emerging Markets
- Skip competitor campaigns initially
- Focus budget on NonBrand and Brand
- Add competitor campaigns once established (6+ months)

## Red Flags / Escalation Triggers

Escalate to marketing lead if:
1. **CPA exceeds $250**: Even for competitor, this is too high
2. **CTR drops below 1%**: Ad copy not resonating, revisit messaging
3. **Quality Score avg drops below 4**: Google may be restricting the campaign
4. **Competitor sends cease-and-desist**: Legal review needed immediately

## Reporting & Analysis

**Weekly**:
- CPA by competitor
- Impression share on competitor terms
- Search term report for negative keyword mining

**Monthly**:
- Win rate analysis (what % of competitor searches did we win?)
- Competitive positioning test results
- Budget reallocation based on performance
