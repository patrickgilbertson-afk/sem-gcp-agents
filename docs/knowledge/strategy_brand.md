# Brand Campaign Strategy

## Objectives

1. **Impression Share Defense**: Maintain 90%+ impression share on branded terms
2. **Brand Protection**: Capture users actively searching for our brand
3. **Competitor Defense**: Prevent competitor ads from appearing on our brand terms

## Target Metrics

- **Impression Share**: 90%+ (top of page)
- **CTR**: 20%+ (branded terms have high CTR)
- **CPA**: <$40 (highly efficient, users already brand-aware)
- **Conversion Rate**: 15-25% (high intent)
- **Quality Score**: 9-10 (excellent relevance for brand terms)

## Ad Copy Guidelines

### Headlines
**Primary**: Direct, high-intent
- "[Company Name] - Official Site"
- "Get [Product Name] Free Trial"
- "Sign In to [Company Name]"

**Secondary**: Value prop reinforcement
- "AI Code Completion | [Company Name]"
- "Code 10x Faster | [Company Name]"
- "Trusted by 500K+ Developers"

**Tertiary**: Offers/social proof
- "30% Off Pro Plans This Month"
- "SOC 2 Certified | Enterprise Ready"
- "Rated 4.8/5 Stars by Developers"

### Descriptions
- Focus on removing friction ("Sign up in 60 seconds")
- Highlight free trial ("Try free for 14 days")
- Address common queries ("See pricing", "Contact sales")
- Include trust signals ("SOC 2", "500K+ users")

**Example Ad**:
```
[Company Name] Official Site - Free Trial
Code 10x Faster with AI. Trusted by 500K+ Developers.
Try Free for 14 Days. No Credit Card Required. SOC 2 Certified.
```

## Bidding Strategy

**Maximize Impression Share**:
- Goal: 90%+ top-of-page impression share
- Set high bids to ensure top position
- Accept higher CPC for brand defense

**Automated Bidding**:
- Use Target Impression Share bidding
- Set 90% top-of-page target
- Max CPC cap at $10 (brand terms should never cost this much, but safety cap)

**Budget Allocation**:
- Brand campaigns should have uncapped budgets (within reason)
- Priority: Never lose impression share due to budget constraints
- Monitor daily spend, but don't artificially limit brand defense

## Competitive Threats

### Conquesting Defense
Competitors may bid on our brand terms. Monitor and respond:

1. **Detection**: Weekly impression share reports
2. **Response**: Increase bids to maintain position 1
3. **Legal**: Consider trademark infringement if egregious
4. **Counter-conquest**: Bid on their brand terms proportionally

### Expected Competitors
- GitHub (Copilot)
- Cursor
- Tabnine
- Codeium
- Amazon Q

## Sync Group Considerations

Brand campaigns are **SYNCED** across geos:
- Same keywords across US, UK, DE, FR, etc.
- Same ad copy (with minor localization for language)
- Coordinated budget allocation
- Unified impression share tracking

**Template Campaign**: Usually US market (largest volume)

**Geo Adjustments**:
- UK: Minor language adjustments ("colour" vs "color")
- DE/FR: May need full translation, but strategy identical
- Emerging markets: May start with lower budgets, but same impression share targets

## Quality Score Optimization

Brand campaigns should have **exceptional** Quality Scores (9-10):

**Expected CTR**:
- Very high (20-30%) because users are searching specifically for us
- If CTR drops below 15%, investigate ad copy or competitive threats

**Ad Relevance**:
- Perfect match (use brand name in headlines and descriptions)
- Mirror search query intent (login, pricing, trial, etc.)

**Landing Page Experience**:
- Send to most relevant page (home, login, pricing, etc.)
- Fast load times
- Mobile-optimized
- Clear CTAs

## Red Flags / Escalation Triggers

Escalate to marketing lead if:
1. **Impression share drops below 85%**: Competitive threat or budget issue
2. **CPA exceeds $50**: Unusual for brand terms, investigate fraud or mistargeting
3. **CTR drops below 15%**: Ad copy issue or competitive interference
4. **Quality Score drops below 8**: Landing page or relevance issue

## Reporting & Analysis

**Weekly**:
- Impression share by geo
- Competitive auction insights
- CPA and conversion rate trends

**Monthly**:
- Brand search volume trends (growing or shrinking?)
- New competitor threats
- A/B test results on ad copy
